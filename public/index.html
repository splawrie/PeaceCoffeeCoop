About Peace Coffee

Peace Coffee is a Co-Op among select cafes in the Raleigh/ Durham area that provides access to specialized blends at affordable prices. By working together we are able to create a wide selection of quality coffee blends that cannot be found anywhere else.

Peace Coffee partners with an organic farm in the island nation of Vanuatu, where the temperature and soil conditions create a unique texture and depth of flavor that cannot be matched. Stop by any of our partner stores to taste the difference!

The Expeditioner

After traveling throughout the Pacific Islands in 2009, Meleha Levine developed a deep love for both mountaineering and local foods and flavors. The Expeditioner was born out a passion to merge both of these loves together here in the United States.

The Expeditioner features a single origin blend from Peace Coffee's Co-Op farm in Vanuatu, as well as a rich and flavorful blend created by pairing with a neighboring farm in Papua New Guinea.

By partnering with Peace Coffee, The Expeditioner is able to harvest quality beans at affordable prices, enabling Levine to fulfill her passion of bringing food and flavors from the Pacific Islands to inquiring hearts and palates here in the Raleigh/Durham area.

5% of proceeds from The Expeditioner go towards helping the Nepalese communities that call Mt. Everest home. A special focus is given to familes of Sherpas who have lost husbands and fathers while endeavoring to assist adventurous souls in scaling this beautiful mountain.

The Groovy Nerd

The place for Geeks, Freaks, Nerds, and plain old oddballs!

The Groovy Nerd came to life in 1976 as the brain child of self-proclaimed geek, Jeff Taylor and his business partner, Sir Groove-A-Lot. Taylor only serves organic Free-Trade Coffee because, “Hey man!”, it ain’t cool to destroy the environment or take advantage of poor communal farmers.

The Groovy Nerd boasts highly specialized brewing techniques birthed from the research of some of the greatest coffee-nerds the world has known! If you're looking for a far out place to hang-loose, drop out, or just simply float away with the best cup of coffee you've tasted, The Groovy Nerd is sure to please. Their specialty coffee “Code Daze” has a cult following and will keep you feeling jazzed up all day long!

5% of proceeds from The Groovy Nerd go towards sustaining communal farming areas in the North Carolina mountains.

The Bus Stop

The Bus Stop is an innovative cafe owned by Gabe Maughn. It boasts a welcoming and ecclectic vibe that cannot be found anywhere else in the Raleigh Durham area.

Maughn's inspiration was birthed after a car malfuntion forced him to take the bus to work for several weeks. True to his outgoing nature, Maughn struck up conversation with fellow weary souls at the bus stop each morning, gaining insight into their lives and passions.

It was there Maughn learned of the struggles ex-cons face when attemping to acquire jobs among the general population. Maughn's heart was struck to create The Bus Stop as a way of helping hardworking ex-cons find well paying jobs that are challenging to find otherwise.

Not only does Maughn provide jobs for inquiring workers, but teaches the ins and outs of coffee roasting and blending. Maughns sees his employees as family, providing not only jobs, but potential careers.

5% of profits from The Bus Stop go to rehabilitation programs for men and women within North Carolina's prison system.

The Coal Miner

The Coal Miner is the quintessential rustic-chic cafe, owned by West Virginia native Stephanie Lawrie.

Born and raised in the coal mining territory of Eastern West Virginia, Lawrie saw first hand the life of struggle and sacrifice endured by the hardworking families of the coal mines.

Although Lawrie now lives in Raleigh, she has never forgotten her roots and frequently returns to WV to reunite with her mother and loved ones.

The Coal Miner was born out of Lawrie's love for her home state and the hardworking familes therein. 5% of Lawrie's profits go straight to coalmining familes in her hometown and surrounding areas.

Superman Central

There is truly no place like Superman Central Cafe. Peace Coffee partner Colin Williams birthed this unique cafe out of his love for superheros and caffeine. As an avid gamer, Williams spent many nights downing his favorite coffee blends while leveling-up on the latest video games. This crazy concept gave rise to one of Raleigh's favorite hang-out spots.

Superman Central boasts a fun and playful atmosphere where patrons can read comic books, play video games and enjoy quality roasts all at once. Cool Factor: If you beat any one of Williwam's beloved video games, your picture goes on the Superhero Wall of Fame!

Superman Central is truly a place for creativity, fun and exploration. As a reflection of William's generous and loving spirit, 5% of proceeds fund an after school program that fosters fine and visual arts skills among underprivilaged youth.

Vanuatu Farming

Peace Coffee has partnered with a fantastic organic coffee farm in the island nation of Vanuatu, located in the Pacific islands.

This beautiful partnership is the result of years of cultivating not only land, but relationships with the citizens of Vanuatu. Dozens of local families are involved in the farming process and are just as passionate about coffee as we are!

Peace Coffee seeks to cultivate local growth by establishing a sense of community, love and understanding. The dedicated partners here at Peace Coffee frequently visit the farm, viewing the people we work with not only as friends, but as family. Great lengths are taken to ensure that Peace Coffee is not overtaking or comendeering the land or culture of the island, but partnering with the existing community in a way that enhances the lives of those who call Vanuatu home.

The unique soil conditions and climate, as well as the great care taken in the cultivation and harvest of the beans, results in a deep and rich coffee bean that is unlike any other.
